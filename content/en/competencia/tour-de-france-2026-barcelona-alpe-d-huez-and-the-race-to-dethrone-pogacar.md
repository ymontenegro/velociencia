## Barcelona Opens the Door to the Tour

On July 4, 2026, the Catalan capital will become the starting point of the 113th edition of the Tour de France. It will be the first time in history that the race begins with a team time trial, a 19-kilometer format governed by Paris-Nice rules where the time of the first rider of each squad determines the classification. Barcelona previously hosted Tour starts in 2009, but this edition promises something different. The first three stages will take place in Catalan territory, with the second day featuring 2,400 meters of elevation gain concentrated in a final circuit in Montjuïc.

Race director Christian Prudhomme described the design of these three weeks as a "crescendo." The word is no accident. The initial stages are contained, mountains appear sparingly in the first half, and the explosion comes at the end, with the two toughest days reserved for stages 19 and 20. This race architecture aims to concentrate the drama in the final days and maintain uncertainty for as long as possible.

## The Route: 3,333 Kilometers of Crescendo

The numbers of this edition reflect a trend consolidating in modern cycling. Only one stage exceeds 200 kilometers, the daily average drops to 158 kilometers compared to 166 in 2024, and the total accumulated elevation gain reaches 54,450 meters spread over 21 stages. There are seven flat days, four medium mountain stages, and eight high mountain stages with five summit finishes. There are two time trials: the opening team time trial in Barcelona and an individual 26-kilometer time trial between Evian and Thonon-les-Bains on stage 16.

The Tourmalet appears on stage 6 as a Pyrenean appetizer, accompanied by the Col d'Aspin before the final descent to Gavarnie-Gèdre. But the Pyrenees will not be the ultimate battleground. That role belongs to the Alps, where stages 19 and 20 concentrate the greatest brutality of the route. Stage 20 offers 5,600 meters of positive elevation with the sequence Croix de Fer, Télégraphe, Galibier, and Col de Sarenne before topping out at Alpe d'Huez for the second consecutive time. It is the most demanding queen stage designed in recent years.

## Pogačar: The Man Who Won It All in 2024

Tadej Pogačar arrives at this edition as the most dominant cyclist of the 21st century. In 2024, he completed cycling’s Triple Crown by winning the Giro d’Italia, the Tour de France, and the World Road Championship in the same season. Only Eddy Merckx in 1974 and Stephen Roche in 1987 had achieved that feat before. Pogačar went further: he added Liège–Bastogne–Liège and the Giro di Lombardia that same year, something no rider had done in combination with the Triple Crown.

The numbers from his 2024 season defy the logic of modern sport. He covered 7,088 kilometers of racing across the three Grand Tours, accumulated 101,400 meters of elevation gain, won 12 stages between the Giro and the Tour, and claimed the rainbow jersey by attacking 101 kilometers from the finish in Zurich. He ended up riding solo for the last 50 kilometers. At 27 years old, he already has four Tour de France titles, one Giro d’Italia, and ten Monument wins.

In 2025, he maintained his level with victories at the Tour of Flanders and Liège–Bastogne–Liège, as well as a fifth consecutive Lombardia, equaling Fausto Coppi’s historic record. Analysts agree that the 2026 route, without cobblestones or gravel sectors that could cause mechanical surprises, favors his profile. The UAE Emirates-XRG team described the course as "calmer and lighter" than previous editions.

## Vingegaard: The Giro-Tour Double as a Weapon

Jonas Vingegaard is the only rider who has shown the ability to beat Pogačar in a Grand Tour. He did so in the 2022 Tour and repeated in 2023, imposing absolute dominance in the high mountains. The severe crash he suffered at the 2024 Itzulia Basque Country sidelined him for the first part of that season, but he returned to contest the 2025 Vuelta a España, where he won decisively.

For 2026, Vingegaard has made a risky but calculated decision. He will race the Giro d’Italia between May and June before tackling the Tour in July. It will be his debut in the Corsa Rosa and an attempt to complete the Grand Tour set. The Giro organizers designed a softer route than usual, precisely to attract figures like the Dane and facilitate the double. Only one rider has completed the Giro-Tour double in the 21st century: Pogačar in 2024.

The logic behind this strategy is that Vingegaard needs demanding competition before the Tour to arrive in peak form. In 2023, when he won his second Tour, he had previously raced Tirreno-Adriatico and several classics. In 2026, he raises the stakes with a three-week race as preparation. The risk is evident: the accumulated fatigue of 42 days of racing between May and July could erode the freshness needed for stages 19 and 20 in the Alps.

## Evenepoel, Roglič, and the Other Contenders

Remco Evenepoel faces his first season with Red Bull-BORA-hansgrohe after leaving Soudal-QuickStep. The Belgian ruled out racing the 2026 Giro, suggesting a full focus on the Tour. Evenepoel won the Olympic time trial and road race in Paris 2024 and finished fourth in the Tour that same year. His pure time trialist profile gives him a specific advantage on stage 16 between Evian and Thonon, where the 26 kilometers with elevation suit his characteristics.

Primož Roglič, three-time Vuelta a España winner, remains a name to consider at 36 years old. His ability in time trials and high mountains keeps him a podium candidate, although the gap with Pogačar and Vingegaard seems to widen each season. At Red Bull-BORA-hansgrohe, he will share the roster with Evenepoel, forcing the team to define a clear hierarchy before July.

Among emerging names, Isaac del Toro deserves attention. The 21-year-old Mexican won the UAE Tour and Tirreno-Adriatico in 2026, confirming a qualitative leap after his second place in the 2025 Giro, where he took the young rider classification. In this Tour, he will debut under Pogačar’s tutelage at UAE Emirates-XRG, a learning process aimed at preparing him to lead his own Grand Tour campaigns in coming years.

## Where the Race Will Be Decided

The time trials add up to just 45 kilometers between the two events, a historically low figure that reduces the margin for specialists. In 2024, the Tour’s time trials totaled 59 kilometers, and Pogačar used them to extend his lead. In 2026, the battle will be fought almost exclusively in the mountains.

Stage 14 in the Vosges, with nearly 4,000 meters of elevation gain and the sequence Grand Ballon – Ballon d’Alsace – Col du Haag, will be the first real test for the yellow jersey favorites. But the final verdict will come in the Alps. Stage 19, with 70 kilometers of plateau before tackling the Col d’Ornon and the final climb to Alpe d’Huez, offers an ideal scenario for long-range attacks. Stage 20, with its 5,600 meters of elevation distributed over four first-category climbs, is the kind of day where Pogačar has proven devastating.

The final stage in Paris also holds a surprise. For the second consecutive year, the route includes a passage through Montmartre, a short but explosive climb that could shake up the general classification if the gaps are tight. It will not be a triumphant parade on the Champs-Élysées as in traditional editions.

## The Tour as a Barometer of an Era

The 2026 Tour de France presents itself as the ultimate test of whether anyone can stop Pogačar on his turf. The Slovenian has won the last two Tours with growing dominance, and his Monument season in spring reinforces the perception of a cyclist without obvious weaknesses. The question is not whether Pogačar is the favorite, but whether the combination of a motivated Vingegaard aiming for the Giro-Tour double, an Evenepoel unleashed in a new team, and a route designed to concentrate excitement can produce a more competitive race than previous editions.

The 3,333 kilometers between Barcelona and Paris will answer that question between July 4 and 26. The only certainty is that Alpe d’Huez, the mountain that has defined so many Tours since 1952, will once again be the stage for the finale. This time, twice over.
